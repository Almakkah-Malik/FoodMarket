PURE SPICES

Spices are important in both cooking and medicine. They improve the natural flavor of cuisines and can be used to adjust the appearance of food and make it more pleasing in color. Some of them grow wild and are an excellent source of food preservatives, especially in industrially processed foods. Spices are composed of fiber, carbohydrate, fat, sugar, protein, gum, ash, or preservative effects. All of these components impart each spice's particular flavor, color, nutritional, health volatile (essential oils), and other nonvolatile components.
